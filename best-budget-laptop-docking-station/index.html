A laptop docking station (or hub) is the simplest way to turn a few USB‑C ports into a full home‑office workstation. You plug in one cable, and suddenly you have an external monitor, a mouse, a keyboard, and maybe Ethernet or an SD card reader. But not every dock needs to cost a fortune. Budget models now offer surprisingly good connectivity for under a few hundred dollars, and they’re ideal for remote workers, students, or anyone who just needs more ports without the premium price tag. When choosing a budget dock, focus on the ports you actually use. Pay attention to video output (HDMI, DisplayPort, or VGA), USB‑A data ports, and whether it supports power delivery pass‑through so your laptop stays charged. Build quality and cable length matter too, especially if you travel.
